## 1. Name — Pallasite

### Etymology

A **pallasite** is a stony-iron meteorite — translucent olivine crystals suspended in a nickel-iron matrix. Pallasites are among the most visually striking objects in the solar system: green crystal lattice glowing inside cold metal. The name is triply-rooted:

1. **Peter Simon Pallas** (1741–1811), the German naturalist who first described the type from a 680 kg meteorite found in Siberia in 1772.
2. **2 Pallas**, the asteroid he is also commemorated by — the second-largest object in the asteroid belt.
3. **Pallas Athena**, Greek goddess of wisdom and strategic warfare.

### Why this name fits

- **Direct asteroid invocation.** The name *is* a kind of meteorite. Genre-evoking without being literal.
- **Hard money, made literal.** Pallasites are iron and nickel from space. Sats are hard money. The metaphor is built into the noun.
- **Travels beyond Asteroids.** It's a celestial object class, not a tribute to a specific 1979 arcade title. The game can evolve.
- **Three classical hits.** The asteroid, the scientist, the goddess — each provides distinct narrative material.

## 3. Tone

### Personality

1. **Sharp** — kinetic, no fluff. Reads like a pilot's radio call.
2. **Sovereign** — the player owns the score, the sats, the name.
3. **Wry** — knowing winks at the genre. Dry humour, classical references.
4. **Cold** — vector-precision, void-of-space aesthetic. Confident silence over cheerleading.

### Voice guide

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use punchy verbs (*ignites, banked, drift, stake*) | Use marketing fluff (*amazing, revolutionary, next-gen*) |
| Speak in numbers and physics (*+240 sats, Wave 4*) | Cheerlead the player (*Awesome job!*) |
| Reference orbital mechanics naturally | Over-explain Bitcoin, Lightning, or Nostr in-game |
| Reward the curious with classical and scientific Easter eggs | Lecture about freedom tech |
| Use British English | Default to US conventions |
| Pair short verb-noun phrases for rhythm | Write paragraphs |
